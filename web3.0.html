Blockchain


Blockchain technology can be described as a decentralized, distributed ledger that records the provenance of a digital asset,
essentially a digital ledger of transactions that is duplicated and distributed across the entire network of computer systems on the blockchain.
Blockchain is a type of DLT in which transactions are recorded with immutable cryptographic signature called a hash.

Smart Contracts

Smart Contracts are programs on the Blockchain that react when certain parameters are met.
Imagine a farmer who wants crop insurance, and the contract will react if a parameter is met e.g.,
if the amount of rainfall in the area of the crop is under a certain amount then the smart contract with data recieved from an oracle would react and release the insurance funds agreed within the Smart Contract.

dApps

Decentralized Applications (dApps) are virtual applications or programs that exist on the blockchain, dApps run on a peer-to-peer network.
Smart Contracts are connected to dApps, some cryptocurrency wallets have dApps integrated within the wallet.

NFT

Non-Fungible Tokens are digital assets mostly minted as quirky artworks,
totally unique and non-interchangeable unit of data stored on a digital ledger that uses blockchain technology to establish proof of ownership.
They are collectable digital assets that value in cryptocurrency (usually Ethereum)
